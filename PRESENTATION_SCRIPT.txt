[Visible on slide: three stat cards — "~739" (Chicago 1995),

Heat is not a weather problem. It's a design problem.

In 1995, a five-day heatwave killed roughly **739 people** in Chicago.
Twenty-seven years later, summer 2022, heat killed **61,672 people**
across Europe — inside one of the most advanced healthcare systems
on earth. GDP did not protect them. Urban form did not protect them.

  [PAUSE after "GDP did not protect them" — the stat needs air.]

We picked three cities to stress-test generalization. Rio, Santiago,
Freetown. Three climate zones. Two are labeled. Freetown is blind —
no ground truth, no calibration. Just the model, the pixels, and
the city.

That's not interpolation. That's extrapolation. And it's the same
problem that exists for almost every African city without a sensor
network.

Rashad will take you through the data.

Five notebooks, one pipeline. Notebooks 1 and 2 search scenes and

SLIDE 5 — 32 FEATURES, FIVE TIERS                  Woo Jin · ~55s
"Thirty-two features across five tiers"
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Visible on slide: five tier rows (Classical spectral · Advanced
 spectral · Physical composites · Building geometry · Geo-aware
 context) with feature counts 3/8/6/7/11. Right panel: "THE
 BREAKTHROUGH — Geo-aware context" with the 0.58 → 0.689 lift.]

Thirty-two features, five tiers.

Tiers one through three are spectral — NDVI, NDBI, NDWI, then
advanced indices, then physical composites like urban_heat and
veg_cooling. Tier four is building geometry from OSM.

Tier five — eleven features — is where the model becomes honest.
These encode *where a pixel sits*. Distance to coast. Distance to
forest. Distance to city center. Local urban fraction in a 500-meter
window. Local vegetation fraction. One-hot land-use class.

  [SLOW DOWN on the next sentence — this is the technical lift
   worth claiming.]

Before this layer, Freetown honest F1 was **0.58**. After: **0.689**.
An eleven-point lift from teaching the model geography.

A pixel in downtown Santiago and a pixel in downtown Freetown can
have identical NDVI. They do not have identical geography. The model
needed both.

SLIDE 6 — TWO DESIGN CHOICES                       Woo Jin · ~40s
"Per-city normalization plus spatial-block cross-validation"
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Visible on slide: two cards side by side — "01 Per-city
 normalization" and "02 Spatial-block cross-validation" — with a
 footer banner "These two choices together lift Freetown honest F1
 by roughly ten points over the naive baseline."]

Two design choices make this work.

**Per-city z-score normalization.** Raw NDVI in arid Santiago is
not raw NDVI in tropical Freetown. After normalization, the model
learns "hotter than this city's own mean" — not absolute thresholds
that break across climates.

**Spatial-block cross-validation.** A random pixel split looks
beautiful and lies — adjacent pixels leak between train and test.
We cluster into 60 geographic KMeans blocks and hold out whole
blocks. CV scores stop lying.

Together, these two choices are worth roughly ten F1 points. Otelima
will cover how we handle Freetown's missing labels.

SLIDE 7 — PHYSICS-ANCHORED PSEUDO-LABELING         Otelima · ~55s
"Physics-anchored stratified pseudo-labeling"
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Visible on slide: four-step flow — 01 RANK · 02 FORCE-BALANCE ·
 03 FILTER · 04 RETRAIN — followed by a "PSEUDO-LABELS GENERATED"
 row with per-city counts (Freetown 7,978 · Santiago 7,229 · Rio
 13,786).]

Freetown has no labels. So how do we give the model Freetown-specific
signal?

Four steps.

**Rank.** Every pixel in the target city gets scored by a physical
heat composite.

**Force-balance.** Top 15% become pseudo-High. Bottom 15%, pseudo-Low.
Middle 15% around the median, pseudo-Medium. Physics picks the class
balance — not model confidence. That matters. Confidence-based
pseudo-labels always collapse to the majority class. Ours cannot.

**Filter.** Keep only labels the current model already predicts at
20% probability or higher.

**Retrain.** Weight 0.30, two rounds.

Result: roughly **8,000 useful pseudo-labels for Freetown**. The
model learns the shape of Freetown without ever being told the
answer.

BACKUP A — "Heat follows airflow" (wind/foehn hypotheses)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Use if asked: "What physical mechanism explains the hot pixels?"

Two testable hypotheses, framed explicitly as hypotheses.

**Hypothesis α — Foehn-override.** Vegetated zones downwind of the
eastern hills still register High UHI. Interpretation: mountain-lee
descending flow may suppress evapotranspirative cooling. Test:
local weather stations × wind-vector overlay.

**Hypothesis β — Wind-corridor × density.** At equal built density,
sea-breeze-facing blocks run cooler than sheltered ones. Street
orientation and LiDAR heights may gate sea-breeze penetration.
Test: building-height model × dominant street azimuth.

Framing note: these are field-testable hypotheses we derived from
map inspection, not conclusions from the model output.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
BACKUP B — "Heat follows form" (urban composition hypotheses)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Use if asked: "What urban-form features explain the variation?"

Three hypotheses (derived from Google Earth overlay inspection,
not model output):

**Hypothesis γ — Impervious-surface composition.** High-asphalt
ratio associates with High-class pixels within "built-up" zones.

**Hypothesis δ — Intersection micro-hotspots.** Local High spots
cluster at major-road intersections — plausibly signal-idling heat.

**Hypothesis ε — Urban form > green cover.** Low-NDVI blocks with
favorable canyon geometry stay in Medium. If confirmed, this
inverts the "plant more trees citywide" recommendation — ship these
as pilots, not policies.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
BACKUP C — "Top 5 Features — Random Forest"

Use if asked: "What actually drives the predictions?"

Horizontal bar chart, values from the RF model:

  1. local_urban_frac_500m ... 0.222
  2. dist_to_center_km ....... 0.170
  3. building_density_100m ... 0.077

One-line headline: *urban form and distance-to-center dominate —
the built environment is the signal, not pure spectral indices.*

Implication: the model agrees with urban-climatology literature
